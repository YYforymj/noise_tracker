Noise Monitor Online

Measure live environmental sound levels in dB directly in your browser.

How to use the tool

  1. Open the page and allow microphone access.
  2. Start monitoring to see live decibel readings.
  3. Compare minimum, average, and maximum noise levels.
  4. Stop the session to review saved history.

Common noise level examples

  • 30 dB: quiet room or whisper
  • 50 dB: normal conversation
  • 70 dB: busy street or vacuum cleaner
  • 85 dB: loud machinery or heavy traffic

Why track noise levels

Monitoring sound levels helps protect hearing, improve sleep, reduce office distractions, and track neighborhood noise over time.

Frequently asked questions

The tool does not record audio. It calculates relative dB levels in real time, and a calibrated external microphone is recommended for precise SPL measurements.
